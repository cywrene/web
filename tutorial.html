Folding Techniques

The following are the main basic techniques that serves as starting point when making origami models.


Valley Fold

This is one of the basic folds where it shows a “V” shape as to why it’s called as valley.
1. To make a valley fold, just fold the paper in half towards you.


Mountain Fold

Another basic fold is mountain fold. This is the pretty similar to valley fold considering this is just its reverse technique.
1. To make a mountain fold, fold part of the paper away from you and then crease along the line.


Bases


These are a short series of folds that can be done in a variety of directions depending on the goal. The most typical bases when learning origami are preliminary, bird and water bomb, and many others. As the name implies, the preliminary base is used in traditional origami such as tulip, iris, star box, iris, etc. It can become a bird base with just a little modification, where it used to create the crane, turtle and others. The water bomb base is found in origami such as the water butterfly, bomb itself, tea plate and more.
